# Business Ideas — Speaking / Coaching / Consulting App (Claude-Assisted Build)

## Business summary

The client runs a business in **professional speaking, coaching, and consulting**. A new app would likely help them **serve clients**, **deliver programs or content**, **book and manage sessions**, and **stay in touch** between engagements. Money may come from **programs, retainers, events, and corporate work**. Using Claude in development speeds iteration; the **product still needs a clear user job** (what the app does better than a website, email, or PDF).

----
## Competitors

**Direct (same job: app for coaches/speakers)**  
- **Kajabi, Teachable, Thinkific** — courses and memberships; strong for content, weaker for deep custom mobile UX unless you build on top.  
- **Practice, Paperbell, HoneyBook-style tools** — scheduling and client admin for coaches.  
- **Circle, Skool** — community (client already uses Skool in history); good for groups, not a full custom brand app.

**Substitutes (different product, same user goal)**  
- **Calendly + Stripe + email** — simple booking and payment without an app.  
- **Notion / Google Drive** — resource delivery without native mobile polish.

----
## Similar business examples

- **Coach apps** that combine **content + calendar + messaging** keep clients inside one habit loop.  
- **Event and keynote clients** often want **one place for agendas, slides, and follow-ups**.  
- **Corporate programs** benefit from **role-based access** (buyer vs participant vs admin).

## Personalized ideas for this project

### Quick wins (apply now)

1. **Define one “hero workflow” for v1**  
   - *Idea:* Pick one path (e.g. book a call → pay → get prep worksheet) and ship only that end-to-end.  
   - *Why:* Stops scope creep when AI speeds up coding.  
   - *Effort:* low | *Impact:* high  

2. **Client admin vs end-user app split**  
   - *Idea:* Separate **coach dashboard** (content, offers) from **client app** (consumption, booking).  
   - *Why:* Matches how speaking/coaching businesses actually run.  
   - *Effort:* medium | *Impact:* high  

3. **Brand and trust layer**  
   - *Idea:* In-app **testimonials, speaker bio, and clear refund/session policy**.  
   - *Why:* Consulting buyers need proof and safety.  
   - *Effort:* low | *Impact:* medium  

### Mid-term improvements

4. **AI features that fit the industry**  
   - *Idea:* **Session prep summaries**, **talk outline generator**, or **feedback digest** — powered by API, not just “chat in the app.”  
   - *Why:* Ties “AI app” to measurable client outcomes.  
   - *Effort:* medium | *Impact:* high  

5. **Integrations**  
   - *Idea:* Calendar (Google/Microsoft), Stripe, and optional **Skool or community link** in one settings area.  
   - *Why:* Reduces duplicate work for the team.  
   - *Effort:* medium | *Impact:* medium  

### Growth ideas (scale and differentiation)

6. **Corporate “license” mode**  
   - *Idea:* Organizations buy seats; managers see completion and engagement.  
   - *Why:* Speaking/consulting revenue often scales through companies.  
   - *Effort:* high | *Impact:* high  

7. **Offline-first content for travel**  
   - *Idea:* Download key videos/PDFs for flights and poor hotel Wi‑Fi.  
   - *Why:* Speakers and clients are often mobile.  
   - *Effort:* medium | *Impact:* medium

## Clarifying questions (if details are missing)

1. Is the app mainly for **B2C (individual coachees)** or **B2B (companies)**?  
2. Do you need **in-app payments and subscriptions**, or is payment handled elsewhere?  
3. Is **video** core (Zoom-like), or is the app mostly **content + scheduling**?  
4. Must the product live under **your brand in the App Store**, or is a **white-label web + PWA** acceptable for v1?
