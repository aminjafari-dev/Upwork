# Business summary
MyProgrix is a tiny mobile product for golfers who need quick reset moments before the next shot. The user opens the app, starts one tap flow, gets a haptic cue, and exits. The value is reliable execution under stress, not feature depth. Revenue is subscription-based, so trust, feel consistency, and store compliance drive retention.

Assumptions to confirm before final product strategy:
1) Is initial launch for solo golfers only, or coaches too?
2) Will pricing be monthly only, or monthly + annual?
3) Is branding already final for store assets and paywall copy?
4) Is there a target handicap range for first users?

----

# Competitors
- **Decade Golf / performance apps (indirect):** same user goal (better focus and shot outcomes), but heavy feature sets.
- **Calm/Breathing apps (substitute):** quick state reset use case, but wrong framing for this product and not golf-first.
- **WHOOP/Oura readiness habits (indirect):** same outcome intent (steady performance state), different delivery model.
- **Simple haptic timer apps (direct substitute):** closest behavior pattern, but usually weak in premium feel and reliability proofs.

----

# Similar business examples
- **Niche single-job tools:** products that solve one repeated micro-moment win because the experience is fast and dependable.
- **Minimal ritual products in sport:** tools with strict flow and no extra UI keep usage high during real play.
- **Subscription utilities with low surface area:** conversion is mostly trust + clear value + low friction restore/compliance.
